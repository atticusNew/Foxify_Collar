## Margin model

Deribit `cross_sm` (cross-margin SM) treats LONG options as fully-paid:

- Initial margin = premium paid (USD-denominated, in BTC at the time of purchase)
- Maintenance margin = 0 (unlike futures or short options)
- Cross-margin nets across positions: long puts ↔ long calls partially offset on vol moves

We hedge by BUYING puts (for LONG protection) and BUYING calls (for SHORT protection). We never SELL options, so we never have variation margin or assignment risk on our hedge book. Capital required is roughly the sum of premiums currently paid across all open positions.

**Headroom adders on top of premium-sum:**

- 20% vol-spike buffer (Deribit's recommended cross-margin headroom)
- 10% new-trade headroom (so trade N+1 doesn't trip margin call when N concurrent)
- **Total recommended equity = 1.30 × peak premium-sum**

## Caveats and what this does NOT model

- **Regime dynamics during a concurrent portfolio.** A regime spike during open trades can
  inflate the mark-to-market value of long puts (good) and decay long calls (bad). Cross-margin
  nets but the conservative side dominates short-term margin. The 20% buffer covers ~1-sigma vol
  spikes; not 3-sigma tail events.
- **Withdrawals / deposit timing.** Deribit settles instantly but deposits take 1-2 confirmations.
  Don't run at the floor; keep at least 30-50% slack for operational deposits.
- **PnL on the hedge book itself.** Recovered hedge proceeds (when triggers fire and we sell)
  feed back into account equity. D2 showed mean recovery $610/trade; this is a positive cash
  flow that grows the account over time. We're not modeling that compounding here.
- **Concurrent exposure correlation.** All trades are on BTC. A 5% spot move hits every open
  trade simultaneously. The model assumes independent trades for cost-summation purposes; that's
  the right framing for INITIAL margin (premium-sum) but not for variation in stress.
